Do you need a permit to elope on the beach in Miami? ⌄

Often yes—it depends on the municipality and beach segment. Many public beaches require permits or set rules for ceremonies and photography setups. Your officiant or planner should help you pick compliant spots—or schedule a private terrace backup.

How long does a Florida marriage license take? ⌄

After you apply with the county, there is typically a waiting period before the license is valid for ceremony—exceptions may apply for non-residents. Confirm dates with the clerk and schedule your ceremony inside the valid window.

Can our Miami elopement ceremony be bilingual? ⌄

Absolutely. Many couples blend English and Spanish for vows and storytelling. A bilingual officiant keeps signatures, pacing, and family cues seamless—especially when photos follow immediately after.
